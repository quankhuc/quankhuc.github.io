Artifact 1 · Workshop One

The Evolution of AI & Machine Learning: A Visual Timeline

Embedded preview — open the full timeline in its own page

Title
The Evolution of AI & Machine Learning: A Visual Timeline — eight decades of AI history in one visual, from McCulloch & Pitts (1943) to today's frontier-model race.
Objective
Created for AIML-500 Workshop 1 to answer a question most AI histories gloss over: why did progress stall twice? The timeline is built as an argument, not a list — the two "AI winters" are shaded, the booms are in color, and 2012 (AlexNet) is marked as the hinge where algorithms, hardware, and data finally aligned.
Process
I researched milestones across a documentary history of AI, the IEEE Computer Society's "Evolution of AI," and Our World in Data's "Brief History of AI." I used an AI assistant (Claude) to draft the initial milestone outline, then verified every key date against those two published sources — and caught one date the AI had gotten wrong, which shaped the artifact's own thesis about verification. I then grouped the milestones into eight eras and designed the visual so the boom–winter–boom pattern reads at a glance.
Tools
Hand-coded HTML/CSS (no site builder or template); Claude as a research assistant with source cross-checking against IEEE Computer Society and Our World in Data.
Value
This timeline gives teams and decision-makers a two-minute mental model of AI progress: breakthroughs arrive when algorithms, hardware, and data line up — not from clever ideas alone. That pattern is a practical lens for setting realistic expectations on AI initiatives and reading today's hype cycle against history.
