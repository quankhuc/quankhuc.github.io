The Evolution of AI & Machine Learning

Quan Khuc · AIML-500-01N1C · Workshop One, Discussion 1.3

A boom–bust–boom story: two AI winters separated by breakthroughs that usually weren't a smarter idea: they were the hardware, cloud computing, data, or funding finally catching up to an idea that already existed.

AI Winter: funding/interest collapse Growth & breakthroughs The hinge moment (2012)

Era 1: Foundations (1940s–1956)

1943
McCulloch & Pitts model the artificial neuron
The first math model of how a brain cell might compute: the seed of neural networks.
1950
Turing's "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"
Proposes the Turing Test and asks: can machines think?
1956
The Dartmouth Workshop
John McCarthy coins "artificial intelligence." The field's official birth.

Era 2: Early Optimism (1957–1969)

1958
Rosenblatt's Perceptron
The first neural network that could learn from data, wildly overhyped at the time.
1966
ELIZA (Joseph Weizenbaum)
A chatbot that mimicked a therapist. People bonded with it, which spooked its own creator.

Era 3: The First AI Winter (1970s)

1969
Minsky & Papert publish Perceptrons
Proved single-layer perceptrons couldn't solve basic problems like XOR. Funding and faith collapsed.
1973
The Lighthill Report
A damning UK review that gutted AI funding. The field goes quiet.

Era 4: Expert Systems Boom (1980s)

1980
Expert systems go commercial (XCON, MYCIN)
Rule-based systems captured human expertise: the first time AI made real money.
1986
Backpropagation popularized (Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams)
The training method that finally made multi-layer neural nets learnable. It quietly sets up everything after it.

Era 5: The Second AI Winter (late 1980s–early 1990s)

~1987–1993
Expert systems collapse
They were brittle and expensive to maintain. Money dries up again.

Era 6: Machine Learning Grows Up (1990s–2000s)

1997
Deep Blue beats Garry Kasparov at chess
First time a machine beat a world champion: a huge public moment.
2006
Geoffrey Hinton reframes "deep learning"
Shows deep nets can be trained effectively: the rebrand that starts the comeback.
2009
ImageNet (Fei-Fei Li)
A massive labeled image dataset. Turns out data was the missing ingredient.

Era 7: The Deep Learning Explosion (2012–2016)

Turning point
2012
AlexNet wins ImageNet
Deep nets trained on Nvidia GPUs crush the competition. The algorithms had existed for years. The hardware finally caught up.
2014
GANs (Ian Goodfellow)
Networks that generate realistic fake images: the front edge of generative AI.
2016
AlphaGo beats Lee Sedol at Go
A game everyone assumed was a decade away from being solved.

Era 8: The Generative Era (2017–present)

2017
The Transformer ("Attention Is All You Need")
The architecture behind every modern large language model.
2018–2020
BERT, GPT-2, GPT-3
Language models scale up and start to feel general — powered by cloud computing. Training moved onto huge rented compute clusters (GPT-3 on Microsoft Azure, BERT on Google Cloud TPUs): scale no single lab could own.
2022
ChatGPT launches
100M users in two months. AI stops being a research topic and becomes a household tool.
2023–now
The arms race
OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, and Meta trade blows on frontier models. Corporate rivalry is now the main engine of progress.
